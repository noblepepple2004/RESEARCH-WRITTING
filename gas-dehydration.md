# Gas Dehydration

## Overview

Gas dehydration is the process of removing water vapor from natural gas before transportation or processing.

Water in natural gas can cause operational problems such as corrosion, hydrate formation, and reduced pipeline efficiency.

---

## Why Gas Dehydration Is Important

Removing water helps to:

- Prevent corrosion
- Prevent hydrate formation
- Protect processing equipment
- Improve gas quality
- Meet pipeline specifications

---

## Common Methods

### Glycol Dehydration

The most common industrial method.

Advantages:

- Cost-effective
- Reliable
- Suitable for continuous operation

---

### Solid Desiccant Dehydration

Uses materials such as silica gel or molecular sieves.

Advantages:

- Produces very dry gas
- Used in cryogenic processes

---

## Industrial Applications

Gas dehydration is widely used in:

- Natural gas processing plants
- LNG facilities
- Pipeline transmission systems

---

## Key Takeaways

Effective gas dehydration improves safety, protects equipment, and ensures efficient transportation of natural gas.
